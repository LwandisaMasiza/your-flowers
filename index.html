Protea Flowers

Proteas are ancient, hardy flowering shrubs native to South Africa, renowned for their larg, dramatic and varied flower heads, which can reach 12 inches across. They thrive in sunny, acidic, well-drained soil and are popular as drought-tolerant, low maintenance landscape plants in Mediterranean climates.

Barberton Flower

The Barberton Daisy, also known as the Transvaal Daisy, is a vibrant perennial native to South Africa, specifically Mpumalanga and the Barberton area. They feature rosettes of hairy, lobed green leaves with bright flowers--typically red, yellow, pink or orange on long, upright.

Blushing Bride Flower

The Blushing Bride, is a rare South African shrub from the Proteaceae family, famed for its delicate white to pink papery flowers, blooming from winter to spring. Popular in wedding bouquets, this plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil n full sun. It is historically significant for its 18th -century discovery, presumed extinction, and subsequent rediscovery.
